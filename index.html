Spiti Valley Expedition

Himachal Pradesh · High Himalayas · Road Trip

A slow journey through one of India’s most dramatic landscapes. Long roads, quiet villages, monasteries looking over deep valleys and skies full of stars. Evenings end with hot thukpa, conversations and the kind of silence you only find in the mountains.

Highlights: Key Monastery · Chandratal (seasonal) · Local homestays · High passes

Konkan Coastal Stories

Konkan · Maharashtra · Sea & Villages

Wake up to the sound of the sea, sip cutting chai outside small stations, visit artisan workshops in Sawantwadi, dive in Tarkarli, walk on quiet beaches in Ganpatipule and end the day with fresh Malvani meals. This is Konkan at an unhurried pace.

Highlights: Sawantwadi Palace · Vengurla beaches · Tarkarli scuba experience · Ganpatipule sunset

Temple Trail: Madurai & Rameshwaram

Tamil Nadu · Culture & Heritage

Watch Meenakshi Amman Temple come alive in the evening, wander through ancient corridors, feel the sea breeze in Rameshwaram and listen to stories that connect mythology, history and the present day.

Highlights: Meenakshi Amman Temple · Ramanathaswamy Temple · Local food walks

Little Stories from the Road

Every group, every journey leaves behind small stories – a shared joke in a bus, a surprise viewpoint, a plate of food that everyone keeps talking about.

The Night Sky in Spiti

One of our Spiti groups spent an hour lying on the terrace of a homestay, naming constellations they didn’t know and watching shooting stars. No music, no phones – just cold air, warm chai and sky. Most of them still talk more about that night than any viewpoint.

Konkan Breakfast Conversations

In Konkan, mornings often start slow. On one trip, a “quick breakfast” in a small village turned into a long chat with the homestay family about mango orchards, monsoons and how the sea changes with each season.

Singapore after the Lights

After watching the light show at Marina Bay, a group decided to skip the mall and sat by the water instead, talking about their first trips abroad. Sometimes, the best part of the itinerary isn’t written on paper.
